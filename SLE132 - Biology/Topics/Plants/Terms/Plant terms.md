##### Petiole
<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Petiole</mark> :: The stalk attaching the leaf to the stem

It allows the leaf to obtain the most sunlight by rotating

##### Lignin
<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Lignin</mark> :: An extremely stable polymer that is a major component of plant cell walls and [[Sclerenchyma]] cells/tissues, providing strength and a water-tight exterior
<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Lignification</mark> :: The transformation of a cell wall into lignin [Why does lignification occur? (read conclusion)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4648457/)

Lignin comprises over $\frac{1}{4}$ of the mass of dried wood.
It is present in all vascular plants.

##### Cortex 
Cortex :: When a cell is external to vascular tissue (i.e. external to the phloem/xylem)

##### Pith 
Pith :: When a cell is internal to vascular tissue (i.e. internal to the phloem/xylem)
